Chapter 10

The next day felt longer than it should have.

Do-hyun blamed the clock.

The clock blamed nobody.

It simply continued ticking.

Slowly.

Painfully.

As if it enjoyed making him wait.

Not that he was waiting for anyone.

At least that was what he told himself.

Unfortunately, he wasn't very convincing.

The bell above the café door chimed.

Do-hyun looked up instantly.

A customer.

Not Seo-yul.

He looked away.

Annoyed.

Mostly with himself.

Several more customers came and went.

Still nothing.

By the afternoon, Do-hyun had convinced himself Seo-yul wasn't coming.

Then the bell rang again.

And there he was.

Seo-yul.

Standing in the doorway with that familiar smile.

The one Do-hyun pretended not to notice.

The one he always noticed.

"Miss me?"

"No."

Seo-yul laughed immediately.

"You're getting worse at lying."

"I'm getting better."

"Definitely worse."

Do-hyun rolled his eyes.

The smile threatening to appear ruined the effect.

Seo-yul looked far too pleased about that.

A few hours later, Do-hyun's shift finally ended.

And just like the night before, Seo-yul was waiting.

Ready to leave together.

Neither of them acknowledged how quickly this had become normal.

Neither of them wanted to.

The evening air was cool.

A gentle breeze moved through the streets.

For a while they simply walked.

Talking about random things.

Favorite foods.

Movies.

Embarrassing childhood stories.

The conversation never seemed to stop.

It flowed naturally.

Easily.

Comfortably.

At some point they found themselves sitting on a bench overlooking the river.

The same river as before.

The same city lights reflecting across the water.

But tonight felt different.

Closer.

Quieter.

More real.

Seo-yul leaned back against the bench.

"Can I ask you something?"

Do-hyun sighed dramatically.

"That sentence never ends well."

Seo-yul laughed.

"Just answer."

"Fine."

Seo-yul looked toward the river.

Then back at him.

"When was the last time you did something for yourself?"

Do-hyun blinked.

"What kind of question is that?"

"A normal one."

"No it isn't."

"It is."

Do-hyun opened his mouth.

Then closed it again.

Because surprisingly...

He didn't have an answer.

Seo-yul noticed immediately.

His expression softened.

"See?"

"I hate when you're right."

"I know."

The response came far too quickly.

Do-hyun laughed despite himself.

The sound surprised both of them.

Because it was genuine.

Unforced.

Real.

For a moment, neither looked away.

The city lights shimmered around them.

The river moved quietly below.

Everything else seemed to disappear.

Leaving only the two of them.

And the growing realization that neither wanted these nights to end.

Not anymore.

Not when they had become the best part of the day.

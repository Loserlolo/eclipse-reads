Chapter 14

The next day felt strange.

Not bad.

Just strange.

Do-hyun couldn't explain why.

Everything seemed normal.

Customers came and went.

Coffee machines hummed.

Rain tapped softly against the café windows.

Yet something felt off.

The feeling only grew stronger when Seo-yul didn't show up.

Usually he appeared sometime during the afternoon.

Today he didn't.

Do-hyun told himself he didn't care.

Unfortunately, nobody believed him.

Especially Min-jun.

"You're looking at the door again."

"I'm not."

"You literally just did."

"I was checking for customers."

"Sure."

Do-hyun ignored him.

Mostly because arguing would only make things worse.

By evening, Seo-yul still hadn't appeared.

Which shouldn't have bothered him.

But it did.

More than he wanted to admit.

When his shift finally ended, he stepped outside.

The city streets glowed beneath the rain.

People hurried past carrying umbrellas.

Cars splashed through puddles.

Everything felt unusually quiet.

Then he saw him.

Across the street.

Seo-yul.

Relief hit immediately.

Followed by confusion.

Because Seo-yul wasn't alone.

A girl stood beside him.

Laughing at something he said.

Seo-yul smiled back.

The same smile.

The one Do-hyun knew too well.

For some reason his chest tightened.

Hard.

Painfully.

He didn't understand why.

Or maybe he understood perfectly.

And that was the problem.

Before Seo-yul could notice him, Do-hyun turned away.

Immediately.

The walk home felt longer than usual.

Colder too.

His thoughts refused to leave him alone.

Who was she?

A friend?

A girlfriend?

Someone important?

The questions followed him all night.

The worst part wasn't not knowing.

The worst part was realizing how much he cared.

Because he shouldn't care.

He really shouldn't.

Yet somehow...

He did.

A lot.

And for the first time since meeting Seo-yul, Do-hyun wasn't sure what to do with that feeling.

Chapter 11

The next few days passed quickly.

Too quickly.

At least that was what Do-hyun thought.

Every day seemed to follow the same pattern.

Work.

Coffee.

Customers.

And Seo-yul.

Especially Seo-yul.

The café no longer felt empty when business slowed down.

Because sooner or later, the bell above the entrance would ring.

And Seo-yul would appear.

Usually smiling.

Usually teasing him.

Always staying longer than necessary.

Not that Do-hyun minded.

He just refused to admit it.

The afternoon sunlight poured through the windows as Do-hyun cleaned the counter.

The bell chimed.

He looked up automatically.

Seo-yul.

Of course.

A grin immediately spread across Seo-yul's face.

"Wow."

Do-hyun frowned.

"What?"

"You looked up really fast."

"I was checking for customers."

"Sure."

"I was."

"Liar."

Do-hyun rolled his eyes.

Seo-yul looked far too pleased with himself.

As usual.

Hours later, Do-hyun's shift finally ended.

The two of them left together.

Again.

The routine had become so normal that neither questioned it anymore.

The evening air was cool.

Streetlights flickered on one by one.

The city glowed around them.

For a while they simply walked.

Talking about nothing important.

Yet somehow those conversations always felt important anyway.

Seo-yul suddenly stopped.

Do-hyun nearly walked into him.

"What are you doing?"

Seo-yul pointed toward a small convenience store.

"I'm hungry."

"You stopped walking for snacks?"

"Obviously."

The familiar word made both of them laugh.

A few minutes later they sat outside with drinks and snacks.

The city buzzed softly around them.

For once, neither seemed eager to fill the silence.

It wasn't uncomfortable.

It was easy.

Comfortable.

The kind of silence that only existed between people who enjoyed each other's company.

Seo-yul stared up at the evening sky.

Then spoke quietly.

"You know..."

Do-hyun looked over.

"What?"

A small smile appeared on Seo-yul's face.

"I think this is my favorite part of the day."

The words hit harder than they should have.

For a moment Do-hyun couldn't think of a response.

Because the truth was...

It was his favorite part too.

He just wasn't brave enough to say it.

Not yet.

So instead he looked away.

Trying to hide the smile threatening to appear.

Unfortunately, Seo-yul noticed anyway.

He always did.

And somehow that didn't bother Do-hyun anymore.

Not even a little.

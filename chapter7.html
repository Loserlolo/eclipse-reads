Chapter 7

The next few days settled into a strange routine.

One that Do-hyun definitely wasn't paying attention to.

At least that's what he kept telling himself.

Yet somehow he always noticed when Seo-yul walked through the café doors.

Every single time.

It usually happened around the same hour.

Late afternoon.

Just when the rush began to slow down.

Just when Do-hyun finally had time to think.

Which was unfortunate.

Because thinking lately seemed to involve Seo-yul far more than it should.

The bell above the entrance chimed.

Do-hyun looked up automatically.

And there he was.

Again.

Seo-yul grinned the moment their eyes met.

"Miss me?"

"No."

The answer came so quickly that Seo-yul laughed.

"That wasn't convincing at all."

"It wasn't supposed to be."

"Sure."

Do-hyun rolled his eyes.

Somehow Seo-yul always managed to act like he owned the place.

Not in an annoying way.

Just...

Comfortably.

As if he belonged there.

As if he'd been coming for years instead of a few weeks.

Seo-yul took his usual seat by the window.

The one he'd somehow claimed without ever actually saying it.

Outside, the city moved normally.

Cars passed.

People hurried down sidewalks.

Life continued.

Inside the café, however, things felt different.

At least to Do-hyun.

Every now and then he'd glance toward the window.

And every now and then he'd discover Seo-yul already looking back.

It was ridiculous.

Completely ridiculous.

Yet neither of them seemed willing to stop.

Hours passed.

Eventually Do-hyun's shift ended.

He removed his apron and headed for the employee room.

When he returned a few minutes later, Seo-yul was waiting near the counter.

Which immediately made him suspicious.

"What do you want?"

Seo-yul looked offended.

"Maybe I just wanted to say goodbye."

"You've never done that before."

"There's a first time for everything."

Do-hyun narrowed his eyes.

Seo-yul laughed.

Then his smile softened slightly.

"Actually..."

The change in tone caught Do-hyun off guard.

"What?"

Seo-yul hesitated.

For once he seemed unsure of himself.

A rare sight.

"Do you want to walk with me?"

The question hung between them.

Simple.

Casual.

Yet somehow it felt much bigger than that.

Do-hyun stared at him.

His heart immediately started misbehaving.

Again.

He really needed it to stop doing that.

Unfortunately it had never listened to him before.

Seo-yul rubbed the back of his neck.

"If you're busy, forget it."

"I'm not busy."

The response came faster than intended.

Both of them noticed.

Seo-yul smiled.

A real smile.

Not teasing.

Not playful.

Just happy.

And for reasons Do-hyun couldn't explain, seeing that expression made his chest feel strangely warm.

"Then let's go."

Together, they stepped outside.

The evening air was cool.

The city lights glowed around them.

And neither of them realized it yet.

But this simple walk was about to change everything.

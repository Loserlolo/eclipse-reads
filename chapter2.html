Chapter 2

"Obviously."

Seo-yul laughed.

The sound was warm. Far warmer than the coffee machines humming behind the counter.

"Good to know."

Do-hyun frowned.

"Why?"

Seo-yul shrugged as he stepped further inside. Rainwater dripped from his jacket onto the floor.

"Because I'd be pretty embarrassed if I came all the way here and found out you left."

Do-hyun stared at him.

"You came here for me?"

The words escaped before he could stop them.

Seo-yul paused.

For the first time all night, his confidence seemed to crack.

"Maybe."

The answer wasn't nearly clear enough.

Unfortunately, it was enough to make Do-hyun's heart start acting stupid again.

Outside, thunder rolled across the sky.

The rain somehow grew heavier.

Neither of them spoke for a moment.

Then Seo-yul smiled.

"So..."

"Are you going to keep staring at me?"

"Or are you going to make me a coffee?"

Do-hyun immediately looked away.

"I wasn't staring."

"You were."

"I wasn't."

"You definitely were."

The grin on Seo-yul's face only got bigger.

And somehow that made everything worse.

Or maybe better.

Do-hyun still hadn't decided.

With a sigh, he grabbed a clean cup and turned toward the espresso machine.

The familiar sounds of grinding beans and steaming milk filled the silence between them.

Normally, he liked those sounds.

Tonight they only made him more aware of the fact that Seo-yul was still standing there.

Watching him.

"You're doing it again."

Do-hyun nearly dropped the cup.

"What?"

"Overthinking."

Seo-yul leaned against the counter.

"You get this look whenever you're thinking too much."

"I do not."

"You do."

"I really don't."

Seo-yul laughed again.

"There it is."

"There what is?"

"That face."

Do-hyun hated that Seo-yul could read him so easily.

He hated it even more because he was right.

The coffee machine hissed softly.

Rain continued to strike the windows.

For some reason the entire café felt smaller than usual.

Smaller.

Warmer.

More dangerous.

Not because of the storm outside.

Because of the person standing in front of him.

A few minutes later, Do-hyun slid the finished drink across the counter.

Seo-yul picked it up.

Their fingers brushed.

Only for a second.

Yet somehow that single second felt much longer.

Both of them froze.

Neither said anything.

Neither moved away immediately either.

Then Seo-yul smiled.

A smaller smile this time.

One that looked almost nervous.

And for the first time since he'd walked through the door, Do-hyun realized he wasn't the only one affected by this.

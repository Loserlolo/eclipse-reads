Chapter 1

Do-hyun hated slow nights.

Busy nights gave him something to focus on. Orders piled up, customers complained, and there was never enough time to think.

Slow nights were different. Slow nights left him alone with his thoughts.

And lately those thoughts always seemed to circle back to the same person.

Seo-yul.

The café was quiet enough that he could hear the clock ticking above the register.

He hated that.

Because silence always made him think.

And thinking always led back to Seo-yul.

The rain had started nearly an hour ago.

At first it had been a light drizzle tapping softly against the café windows. Now it was pouring.

Water raced down the glass in endless streams, blurring the city lights outside into streaks of gold, white, and blue.

Most people hated rainy nights.

Do-hyun preferred them.

Rain kept people away. Rain made the streets quieter. Rain gave him an excuse to stay inside and pretend the rest of the world didn't exist.

The clock above the register ticked loudly.

Tick. Tick. Tick.

Each second seemed to stretch longer than the last.

Only two customers remained in the café.

A college student asleep over an open textbook.

And an exhausted office worker scrolling through his phone while nursing a coffee that had gone cold half an hour ago.

Neither looked like they planned on talking.

Fine by him.

Do-hyun wiped down the counter for the third time. Then the fourth. Then the fifth.

Anything to stay busy. Anything to avoid thinking.

Unfortunately, thinking always found him anyway.

Especially on nights like this.

Especially when the rain sounded exactly like it had that night.

The night he met Seo-yul.

The bell above the entrance suddenly chimed.

His heart skipped before he could stop it.

The door slowly swung open.

A rush of cool air swept through the café.

Rain followed.

Then the figure stepped into the light.

Seo-yul.

Of course it was Seo-yul.

Standing there like he'd walked straight out of Do-hyun's thoughts.

His dark hair was damp from the rain.

His jacket was soaked.

And somehow he was smiling.

Again.

That stupid smile.

The one Do-hyun couldn't stop noticing.

Seo-yul lifted a hand.

"You're still here?"

The question was simple.

Yet somehow Do-hyun forgot every response he'd ever learned.

Because all he could think about was the fact that Seo-yul had come back.

"Obviously."

Seo-yul laughed.

The sound was warm. Far warmer than the coffee machines humming behind the counter.

And for reasons Do-hyun refused to examine too closely, hearing that laugh again made the rainy night feel a little less lonely.

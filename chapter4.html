Chapter 4

The emergency lights cast long shadows across the café.

Outside, the storm continued without mercy.

Rain hammered against the windows.

Thunder shook the glass every few minutes.

Neither Do-hyun nor Seo-yul seemed eager to leave.

Not that they could.

The streets outside looked more like rivers than roads.

Seo-yul took another sip of his coffee.

"It's actually pretty good."

Do-hyun raised an eyebrow.

"Actually?"

Seo-yul grinned.

"I had to make sure your ego didn't get too big."

Do-hyun rolled his eyes.

For some reason, though, he found himself smiling.

Just a little.

Seo-yul noticed immediately.

"There it is."

"There what is?"

"That smile."

Do-hyun's smile vanished instantly.

"I don't know what you're talking about."

"Liar."

The word was playful.

Far too playful.

Do-hyun hated how comfortable Seo-yul could make a conversation feel.

They fell into silence again.

But this silence felt different.

It wasn't awkward.

It wasn't uncomfortable.

It felt easy.

Like they had done this before.

Like they belonged here.

Together.

The thought caught Do-hyun off guard.

He quickly looked away.

Seo-yul was already watching him.

Again.

"What?"

"Nothing."

"You're staring."

The irony almost made Seo-yul laugh.

"Maybe I am."

Do-hyun blinked.

That wasn't the response he expected.

For the first time all night, he didn't know what to say.

Seo-yul looked down at his coffee.

His smile faded slightly.

"Can I tell you something?"

The sudden seriousness in his voice made Do-hyun pause.

"Sure."

Seo-yul stared into his cup for several seconds.

Then he spoke.

"I don't usually come back to places."

Do-hyun frowned.

"What does that mean?"

Seo-yul shrugged.

"It means I normally leave."

"I don't get attached."

The words were simple.

Yet something about them felt heavy.

Honest.

Real.

Do-hyun wasn't sure why.

But hearing that made his chest feel strangely tight.

Because despite everything...

Seo-yul had come back.

Again.

And again.

And suddenly Do-hyun couldn't stop wondering why.

Outside, lightning flashed across the sky.

For a brief second, the entire café glowed white.

When the light disappeared, neither of them had looked away.

Neither of them seemed capable of it.

And for the first time, the distance between them felt much smaller than before.

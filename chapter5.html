Chapter 5

The storm raged on outside.

Rain streaked across the windows in silver lines.

Thunder rolled through the city.

Yet neither of them moved.

Neither of them looked away.

The moment stretched.

Long.

Quiet.

Dangerous.

Do-hyun was the first to break eye contact.

His heart was beating far too fast.

He hated that.

Especially because Seo-yul looked completely calm.

Or at least he appeared calm.

The slight tension in his shoulders suggested otherwise.

"You think too much."

Seo-yul's voice was softer than before.

Do-hyun sighed.

"You keep saying that."

"Because it's true."

"Maybe."

Seo-yul smiled.

"There it is."

"There what is?"

"A real answer."

Do-hyun rolled his eyes.

For some reason, that only seemed to amuse Seo-yul more.

The emergency lights continued to glow softly above them.

The café felt smaller than ever.

Warmer.

Safer.

Which made absolutely no sense.

Do-hyun wasn't used to feeling comfortable around people.

Especially not this quickly.

Yet somehow talking to Seo-yul felt easy.

Too easy.

The realization unsettled him.

Seo-yul seemed to notice.

Again.

"You do that a lot."

"What?"

"Disappear into your own head."

Do-hyun looked away.

"Maybe."

Another laugh.

Warm.

Quiet.

Dangerously nice.

Outside, lightning flashed.

For a brief moment the entire café glowed white.

Then darkness returned.

Neither of them spoke.

The silence settled around them.

Not awkward.

Not uncomfortable.

Just quiet.

A comfortable kind of quiet.

The kind Do-hyun didn't remember having with anyone before.

Eventually Seo-yul stood.

Do-hyun's stomach immediately dropped.

The reaction surprised him.

Even more surprising was the disappointment that followed.

"You leaving?"

The question escaped before he could stop it.

Seo-yul paused.

A smile slowly appeared.

"Do you want me to?"

Do-hyun immediately regretted speaking.

Seo-yul laughed.

"There it is again."

"What?"

"That face."

"I hate you."

"No you don't."

Unfortunately...

Seo-yul was right.

Outside, the storm finally began to weaken.

The rain softened.

The thunder grew distant.

Morning was still hours away.

Yet somehow it felt like something had changed.

Something important.

Neither of them knew exactly what it was.

But both of them felt it.

And neither of them would forget this night anytime soon.

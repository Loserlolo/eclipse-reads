Chapter 6

Morning arrived far too quickly.

Sunlight streamed through the café windows.

The storm was gone.

The streets were still wet, but the sky had returned to a bright shade of blue.

Everything looked normal again.

At least on the outside.

Do-hyun stood behind the counter pretending to organize cups.

Pretending being the important word.

Because his thoughts had been a complete mess since last night.

Every conversation.

Every smile.

Every glance.

For some reason he couldn't stop replaying them.

The bell above the entrance chimed.

His head immediately snapped up.

A customer walked in.

Not Seo-yul.

Do-hyun looked away.

Immediately annoyed with himself.

Why had he looked up so fast?

Why was he expecting him?

The answer was obvious.

He just didn't want to admit it.

The day continued.

Customers came and went.

Coffee orders piled up.

Time passed.

Still no Seo-yul.

By late afternoon Do-hyun had almost convinced himself he didn't care.

Almost.

Then the bell rang again.

And this time it really was him.

Seo-yul stepped inside.

Dry.

Relaxed.

Smiling.

Of course he was smiling.

Do-hyun hated how much relief he felt.

"You're back."

The words escaped before he could stop them.

Seo-yul blinked.

Then his smile widened.

"Wow."

"What?"

"You sound happy to see me."

"I'm not."

"Liar."

Do-hyun groaned.

Seo-yul laughed.

The familiar sound filled the café.

And somehow everything felt easier again.

As if last night had changed something between them.

Neither of them knew exactly what.

But it was there.

Small.

Quiet.

Growing.

And neither of them could ignore it forever.

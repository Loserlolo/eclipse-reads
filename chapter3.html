Chapter 3

The rain showed no signs of stopping.

If anything, it seemed determined to get worse.

Thunder rolled across the sky every few minutes, rattling the café windows.

The last customer finally left.

The college student gathered their books.

The office worker tossed his empty cup into the trash and disappeared into the storm.

Then there were only two people left.

Do-hyun.

And Seo-yul.

The realization made Do-hyun strangely aware of everything.

The quiet hum of the refrigerators.

The soft rain against the windows.

The warmth spreading through the café.

And Seo-yul sitting only a few feet away.

"You're staring again."

Do-hyun nearly choked.

"I am not."

"You are."

"I'm literally cleaning."

Seo-yul glanced at the cloth in Do-hyun's hand.

The cloth hadn't moved in almost thirty seconds.

"Very productive cleaning."

Do-hyun rolled his eyes.

"You're impossible."

"And yet you keep talking to me."

Unfortunately, Seo-yul had a point.

Outside, another flash of lightning illuminated the street.

A second later came the thunder.

Loud.

Close.

The lights flickered.

Both of them looked up.

The lights flickered again.

Then everything went dark.

For a moment neither spoke.

The only sound was the rain.

Then:

"...Well."

Seo-yul's voice broke the silence.

"I think your café just died."

Do-hyun groaned.

"Perfect."

The emergency lights clicked on.

They weren't bright.

Just enough to paint the room in a soft golden glow.

Enough to make everything feel different.

Smaller.

More private.

More dangerous.

Do-hyun grabbed his phone and turned on the flashlight.

Seo-yul did the same.

Their beams crossed.

For a second they both laughed.

The sound echoed through the empty café.

And for the first time all night, the tension eased.

Just a little.

"Looks like you're stuck here."

Seo-yul glanced toward the storm outside.

"I think we're both stuck here."

Do-hyun looked through the rain-covered windows.

He hated admitting it.

But Seo-yul was right.

Neither of them was going anywhere anytime soon.

And somehow...

The thought didn't bother him as much as it should have.

Not even close.

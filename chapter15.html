Chapter 15

Do-hyun slept terribly.

Not that he planned to admit why.

Every time he closed his eyes, he saw the same thing.

Seo-yul standing in the rain.

Laughing.

Smiling.

With her.

The image refused to leave him alone.

By the time morning arrived, he was exhausted.

And annoyed.

Mostly with himself.

The entire situation was ridiculous.

Seo-yul could spend time with whoever he wanted.

It wasn't Do-hyun's business.

It definitely wasn't his business.

Unfortunately, his heart disagreed.

Strongly.

The café felt unusually loud that day.

Every sound seemed sharper.

Every minute felt longer.

And Seo-yul still hadn't shown up.

Not during lunch.

Not during the afternoon.

Not even in the evening.

Which somehow made everything worse.

"You're sulking."

Do-hyun looked up.

Min-jun stood nearby holding a coffee cup.

"I'm not sulking."

"You absolutely are."

"I'm working."

"You're aggressively working."

Do-hyun sighed.

Min-jun smirked.

The conversation should have ended there.

Unfortunately, it didn't.

"Did you two fight?"

"What?"

"You and Seo-yul."

"We didn't fight."

"Then what's wrong?"

Nothing.

Everything.

Do-hyun wasn't sure.

Thankfully, the bell above the café door chimed.

Saving him from answering.

His heart reacted before his brain could.

Seo-yul.

Standing in the doorway.

The familiar sight should have made him happy.

Instead, it made everything complicated.

Because now he didn't know how to act.

Seo-yul immediately noticed.

His smile faded.

Only slightly.

But enough.

"Hey."

"Hey."

The conversation felt different.

Awkward.

Uncomfortable.

Wrong.

Seo-yul frowned.

"Did I do something?"

"No."

The answer came too quickly.

Far too quickly.

Apparently Seo-yul thought so too.

Because his expression only became more confused.

The rest of the shift dragged painfully.

When it finally ended, Seo-yul waited outside.

Just like always.

Except this time neither of them knew what to say.

After several moments, Seo-yul broke the silence.

"Okay."

"What?"

"You're definitely upset."

"I'm not."

"You are."

"I'm not."

"You've said three words all day."

Do-hyun looked away.

Seo-yul sighed.

Then suddenly laughed.

A genuine laugh.

The kind that made absolutely no sense.

"What?"

Seo-yul shook his head.

"You saw me yesterday."

The words hit immediately.

Do-hyun froze.

Seo-yul pointed at him.

"There it is."

"There what is?"

"That face."

Do-hyun groaned.

Seo-yul laughed again.

Then smiled.

Softly.

"That was my cousin."

Silence.

Complete silence.

Do-hyun wanted the ground to swallow him whole.

Immediately.

Seo-yul's grin widened.

"Oh my God."

"Don't."

"Were you jealous?"

"Absolutely not."

"You were."

"I wasn't."

"You definitely were."

The smile on Seo-yul's face became impossible to ignore.

And somehow...

For the first time all day...

Do-hyun found himself smiling too.

Even if he was completely embarrassed.

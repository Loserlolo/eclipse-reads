Chapter 12

The problem wasn't Seo-yul.

The problem was everyone else.

More specifically, everyone else had started noticing him.

Do-hyun realized this the moment he walked into work.

His coworker Min-jun took one look at him and immediately smirked.

That was never a good sign.

"What?"

Min-jun's grin widened.

"Nothing."

"You're lying."

"I haven't even said anything yet."

"Exactly."

Min-jun laughed.

Unfortunately, Do-hyun knew that laugh.

It meant trouble.

The morning passed normally.

Coffee orders.

Customers.

The usual routine.

Everything remained peaceful.

Until the bell above the café door chimed.

Do-hyun looked up automatically.

A mistake.

A huge mistake.

Because Seo-yul had just walked in.

And Min-jun noticed.

Immediately.

The silence beside him became suspiciously loud.

Do-hyun didn't even need to look.

He already knew what expression his coworker was making.

"Don't."

"I didn't say anything."

"You were about to."

"I was."

Do-hyun groaned.

Seo-yul approached the counter.

Completely unaware.

Or maybe pretending to be unaware.

That was always a possibility.

"Hey."

His smile appeared instantly.

The familiar one.

The dangerous one.

The one Do-hyun had started looking for without realizing it.

"Hey."

The response came naturally.

Too naturally.

Min-jun nearly choked trying not to laugh.

Seo-yul raised an eyebrow.

"What happened to him?"

"Nothing."

"Something happened."

"Nothing happened."

The grin on Seo-yul's face grew wider.

Which somehow made everything worse.

Much worse.

Hours later, after his shift ended, Do-hyun escaped outside.

Or at least he tried to.

Seo-yul was already waiting.

Leaning casually against a nearby wall.

As if he'd been there forever.

As if waiting for Do-hyun was the most normal thing in the world.

Maybe it had become normal.

That thought should have worried him.

Instead it made him smile.

Just a little.

Seo-yul immediately noticed.

"There it is."

"There what is?"

"That smile."

Do-hyun rolled his eyes.

"I regret meeting you."

"No, you don't."

The annoying thing was...

He was right.

They started walking together.

The city lights slowly brightened around them as evening arrived.

Neither seemed in a hurry.

Neither wanted the night to end.

And somewhere behind them, through the café window, Min-jun watched the two of them leave together.

The knowing smile on his face suggested that things were about to become very interesting.

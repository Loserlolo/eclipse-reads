Chapter 8

The city looked different at night.

The crowds were smaller.

The streets were quieter.

The lights reflected off the pavement in streaks of gold and white.

Do-hyun walked beside Seo-yul with his hands shoved into his pockets.

Neither of them spoke for the first few minutes.

The silence wasn't awkward.

Just unfamiliar.

This was the first time they'd been together outside the café.

Without coffee machines.

Without customers.

Without excuses.

"So."

Seo-yul finally broke the silence.

"So?"

"Do you always look this serious?"

Do-hyun groaned.

"There it is."

"There what is?"

"You making fun of me."

Seo-yul laughed.

"Maybe a little."

"A little?"

"Okay. A lot."

Do-hyun rolled his eyes.

For some reason, though, he couldn't stop smiling.

They continued walking.

Passing small shops.

Streetlights.

Late-night restaurants.

The city buzzed quietly around them.

"Can I ask you something?"

Do-hyun glanced at him.

"Depends."

Seo-yul grinned.

"That's not how questions work."

"Ask anyway."

For a moment Seo-yul looked thoughtful.

More thoughtful than usual.

"Why did you become a barista?"

The question caught Do-hyun off guard.

Nobody had asked him that in a long time.

He thought about it.

Really thought about it.

Then shrugged.

"I like making things."

Seo-yul blinked.

"What?"

"Coffee."

Do-hyun looked ahead.

"It sounds stupid."

"It doesn't."

The answer came immediately.

Without hesitation.

Without teasing.

And somehow that made it harder to look at him.

Because Seo-yul sounded genuine.

Dangerously genuine.

The conversation continued.

Slowly.

Naturally.

One question became another.

Stories became laughter.

Minutes became hours.

Neither of them noticed.

Eventually they reached a small overlook above the river.

The city lights reflected across the water.

Beautiful.

Quiet.

Peaceful.

For a moment neither spoke.

They simply stood there.

Side by side.

Watching the lights dance across the surface below.

"This is nice."

Seo-yul's voice was almost a whisper.

Do-hyun nodded.

It was.

More than nice.

The realization hit him suddenly.

He wasn't enjoying the view.

Not really.

He was enjoying being here.

With Seo-yul.

And that thought terrified him far more than it should have.

Beside him, Seo-yul smiled softly.

Completely unaware of the chaos happening inside Do-hyun's head.

Or maybe...

Not completely unaware.

After all, Seo-yul always seemed to notice things nobody else did.

Including him.

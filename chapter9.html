Chapter 9

The walk back should have felt awkward.

For some reason, it didn't.

The conversation came naturally now.

Easier than before.

As if some invisible barrier between them had quietly disappeared.

Neither of them mentioned it.

But both of them noticed.

The city lights reflected off the wet sidewalks as they continued walking.

The night air was cool.

Comfortable.

Quiet.

Seo-yul kicked a small pebble down the sidewalk.

"You know..."

Do-hyun glanced at him.

"That's usually how people start dangerous conversations."

Seo-yul laughed.

"Good. Then pay attention."

"I already regret this."

"Rude."

Do-hyun smiled despite himself.

The sight seemed to make Seo-yul happy.

For a moment neither spoke.

Then Seo-yul's expression softened.

"Can I tell you something?"

The question sounded different this time.

More serious.

Do-hyun immediately noticed.

"Sure."

Seo-yul looked ahead.

Not at him.

At the street.

At the passing lights.

Anywhere except directly at him.

Which was unusual.

Very unusual.

"I moved around a lot growing up."

Do-hyun stayed quiet.

Listening.

"Different schools."

Different neighborhoods.

Different cities."

Seo-yul shrugged.

"I got used to leaving."

The words reminded Do-hyun of something.

A conversation from the stormy night.

'I don't usually come back to places.'

Suddenly it made more sense.

"You never stayed anywhere long enough?"

Seo-yul nodded.

"Pretty much."

There was no self-pity in his voice.

No bitterness.

Just honesty.

For some reason, that made it worse.

Do-hyun found himself wondering what that must have felt like.

Always leaving.

Always starting over.

Always saying goodbye.

"That sounds exhausting."

Seo-yul laughed quietly.

"It was."

For a few moments neither spoke.

The city continued around them.

Cars passed.

People walked by.

Life moved forward.

Yet somehow the moment felt isolated from all of it.

Like they existed inside their own small world.

Then Seo-yul looked at him.

A genuine look.

The kind that made it difficult to look away.

"You're easy to talk to."

Do-hyun nearly tripped.

"What?"

Seo-yul laughed.

"There it is."

"There what is?"

"That face."

"I hate that you keep saying that."

"And I hate that it's true."

Do-hyun groaned.

Seo-yul's grin widened.

The familiar teasing returned.

Yet somehow it felt softer now.

Warmer.

More personal.

Eventually they reached a street corner.

Seo-yul slowed.

"So..."

Do-hyun already knew.

This was where they would part ways.

For tonight, anyway.

Strangely, he didn't want to.

The realization caught him completely off guard.

Seo-yul rubbed the back of his neck.

A habit Do-hyun was beginning to recognize.

"Same time tomorrow?"

The question was casual.

Almost too casual.

But the hopeful look in Seo-yul's eyes wasn't.

Not even a little.

Do-hyun felt his heartbeat stumble.

Then he nodded.

"Yeah."

A smile immediately appeared on Seo-yul's face.

Bright.

Relieved.

Real.

And somehow seeing that smile made Do-hyun's entire day feel worth it.

Neither of them realized it yet.

But this was no longer just about coffee.

And it hadn't been for a while.
